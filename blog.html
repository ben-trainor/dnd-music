Open Mic Fall 2021, A Historic Moment

10-01-21, 9:30PM

What a wonderful night. This was a great open mic, full more than normal of original music that was actually pretty good! I had the privilege of playing a piece I wrote for acoustic guitar, two violins, and cello over the summer. Playing a piece of music like this with real instruments was a dream come true for me; it's not every day an unknown composer like myself gets to conscript some real string players to play some 4 minute piece he wrote in his bedroom in front of a bunch of friends and strangers alike. It was inspired by wedding music and the emotions that remained with me after I had returned home from my friends' ceremony.

Paper Watch: "Grace" (Live at Open Mic)

Video credit: Caleb Atkins

Also, I normally run sound at these events, so I was worried about playing anything more than a solo guitar piece (my normal setlist at these events). But Michael Smith, an old friend with whom I've bonded more recently as a result of our similar passions for music production was there‐ already playing an original of his own‐and able to cover for me. If you listen closely, and I find this somewhat funny, the mix changes and gets better as the piece continues. Most noticeably, the reverb gets really noticeably louder in the latter third.

Anyways, enjoy this music and more music like it to appear on this website in the future!
